Salem, this present day — 1692
Special Report: Deputy Governor Danforth holds the court; accused remain in custody as examinations continue.
By Derek McKnight
Court proceedings observed this day

Today Deputy Governor Danforth sits at the head of the court with grave countenance and steady hand. He receives petitions, hears witnesses, and sets forth the judgment of those who stand accused. The town gathers at the meetinghouse; many look on with fear, some with resolve, as the Judge commands the proceedings without pause.

What hath passed this day

Danforth refuses delay for several who present affidavits and pleas from kin. He declares that the court must not be mocked and that its testimony is to be kept pure from private malice.

The Governor commands that the accused remain in irons when he judges the evidence sufficient to hold them.

Voices and pleas

Petitions arrive from those who claim innocence; widows and friends beseech for mercy. Danforth hears them, yet answers that the court must do its duty.

Weight of the Governor's word

By his voice the town is moved: citizens whisper that the law stands firm, while others murmur that mercy might yet be neglected.

A note on present proceedings

Our reports what is seen and heard at the court.

Observations

Many say that Danforth's steadiness brings order; others say it closes the door to reconsideration.

Town Notices

Those with testimony not yet heard should present themselves at dawn.
