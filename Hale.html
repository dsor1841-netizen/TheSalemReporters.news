Salem, 1692

Reverend Hale Questions Court

From my observations in the town square
Reverend Hale arrives to question the court

I watched Reverend Hale of Beverly arrive in Salem, summoned as one learned to help find witchcraft. I walked among the families he spoke with and carried their words to the meetinghouse.

At first I heard him name signs of witchcraft. I saw him examine the accused and press them with questions; townsfolk gathered and hoped his learning would bring forth truth.

Having sat through several examinations, I noticed his manner change. He told the judges plainly that fear had led many to false conclusions, and that the court should slow its work and seek clearer proof than rumor and panic.

I watched the town stir at his plea. Some took comfort in his counsel; others clung to dread of the devil. The reverend now urges care and mercy for those kept in prison, and I record his words for any who would read and judge for themselves.

Short report for the report. Eyewitness notes gathered in town.
Signed: Derek McKnight
