Trade Activity Slows Following Court Sessions

Filed by Danna Paola Soriano Gomez | Massachusetts Bay Colony

Merchants across the region have reported reduced activity after recent judicial gatherings drew widespread attention. Many stalls remain open, yet customer flow has noticeably declined during peak hours.

Residents traveling between settlements describe an atmosphere of hesitation, where routine exchanges feel cautious and conversations are kept brief. Some traders have begun adjusting schedules to avoid busy periods tied to court announcements.

Officials have not released additional statements regarding economic effects connected to ongoing proceedings.
