Public Opinions & Letters
Abigail Williams
Salem Resident
I say plainly, there is wickedness afoot in this town, and it doth not hide itself well. Those who pretend innocence may yet be servants of the Devil. I have seen with mine own eyes the signs of deceit, and I shall not hold my tongue whilst evil walks freely among us.
John Proctor
Farmer
There is more sin in false accusation than in any quiet wrongdoing. This court feeds upon fear, and the people are too quick to cast blame upon their neighbors. I fear we damn the innocent whilst calling it righteousness.
Reverend Parris
Minister of Salem
The safety of this town rests upon vigilance. We must not grow careless, for the Devil is cunning. If we fail to act, we invite ruin upon our homes and our souls. Better suspicion than silence.
Elizabeth Proctor
Wife
A good name is not easily won, yet it may be lost in but a moment. I pray that truth shall prevail, though it seems burdened beneath the weight of lies. God sees what man refuses to see.
Anonymous
Name Withheld
I dare not speak my name, for fear it be written next upon the list of the accused. There is a strange power in this town now, where a whisper may become a sentence. I pray for silence, yet silence itself feels dangerous.
Mary Warren
Servant
I meant no harm, yet the weight of it all presses upon me. The court listens, but it hears only what it expects. I would tell the truth, yet truth trembles when fear stands beside it.
Anonymous
Concerned Townsperson
This town hath changed. Neighbors look upon one another with doubt, and kindness grows scarce. I wonder if we have lost our way, though we claim to walk in God's light.
